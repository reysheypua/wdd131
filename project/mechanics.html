MECHANICS: Core Psychological Principles & Game Elements

Intrinsic vs. Extrinsic Motivation

Understanding these two forces is crucial. Intrinsic motivation is performing a behavior because you genuinely enjoy it; this is called "mastery", while extrinsic motivation involves external rewards such as "points" and "money".

Definition

Easiest way to classify if motivation comes from within or from an external incentive.

Examples

Extrinsic: Rewards, status.

Intrinsic: Mastery, autonomy, purpose.

Key Mechanics

These are the specific elements used to translate psychological principles into application design.

Progress Bars & Zeigarnik Effect

Progress bars provide instant visual feedback. The "Zeigarnik Effect" is the compulsion to finish incomplete tasks, which a progress bar expertly exploits.

Streaks & Loss Aversion

Streaks build investment. "Loss Aversion" is the psychological bias where the pain of losing something (your streak) is twice as powerful as the pleasure of gaining it.

Variable Rewards

Variable rewards are unpredictable (like a social media feed refresh or a loot box). This uncertainty makes the system highly addictive and engaging due to dopamine release.

Ethics and Dark Patterns

Gamification is a powerful tool and can be misused. **Dark Patterns** are deceptive design techniques used to manipulate users into taking actions that benefit the business rather than the user, often by exploiting cognitive biases like loss aversion or social proof.
